GIR NATIONAL PARK

  • Location: Junagarh District, ‎Gujarat‎, ‎India‎
  • Area :1412 km2 (545.2 sq mi)
  • Established: 1965
    •
Aside from Africa, Gir National Park in Gujarat is the only place on the planet where you can see lions roaming freely in the wild without being hunted. In order to protect the Asiatic Lion, the government designated the vast geographical area of Sasan Gir as a wildlife sanctuary. From a population of approximately 20 lions in 1913, the population has grown to a comfortable 523 lions according to the most recent census in 2015. In the wilderness, there are 106 male lions, 201 female lions, and 213 sub-adult lions.
